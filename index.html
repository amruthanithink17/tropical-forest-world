About Tropical Forest

Tropical forests are one of the most vital and fascinating ecosystems on our planet, covering only about 7% of the Earth’s surface but hosting more than half of all known plant and animal species. These forests are characterized by warm temperatures, high humidity, and abundant rainfall. The dense vegetation supports a complex web of life — from orchids to exotic birds and mammals. These forests also absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, helping regulate climate. Protecting them is essential for biodiversity, global health, and future generations.

Flora & Fauna of Tropical Forests

Tropical forests are home to thousands of plant species including towering trees, vibrant orchids, and dense shrubs. The animal kingdom here is equally diverse, with colorful birds, monkeys, reptiles, and insects creating a complex ecosystem that supports life in abundance.

Conservation Efforts

Conservation of tropical forests is essential to protect biodiversity, combat climate change, and preserve vital resources. Communities and organizations globally work to prevent deforestation, restore degraded lands, and promote sustainability through education and protected zones.
